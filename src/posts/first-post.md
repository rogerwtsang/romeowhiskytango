# hi, welcome to my blog
merry christmas, sort of. as is usual, i'm a little bit late to things. i've been meaning for a while now to carve out some kind of space to talk about stuff on the internet. none of the social media sites really feel right, and they're kind of confusing to me. substack is a little bit closer to what i'm aiming for, but is also confusing to the luddite in me. so in the spirit of learning, i decided to make a website. i don't totally know how you've made it here, because as of this moment i haven't posted it up anywhere and i haven't told many people about it yet. check back once in a while though, and stay as long as you'd like; i hope to do some cool things and post updates here. feel free to communicate somehow, if you so choose. that also requires me to properly implement it, which i'll get to eventually.

which brings me to a good subject for my first post -- neurodivergence! i can share some of the broad, general backstory about my journey. well, my intention is to share the broad, general back story but my actual tendency is to spin off into multiple tangents. i started medication for adhd almost two months ago and i have to say, it's been lifechanging for me. i'm probably autistic on some level too, but so many things about my life suddenly made sense -- i had internalized so many problems as being 'me' problems, when in actuality they were all tied completely to problems of executive dysfunction. all of the things i had struggled with, \[ focus, mood, motivation, drive, identity crisis, achieving], as soon as i was on my own as an adult, were all things i attributed to depression.

boy, did i ever struggle with depression. i consider my first major 'episode' to be the 1B term of my undergrad degree. i was 18 and really struggling, because it really felt like i was drowning, academically. i figured, if i could do really well in school  -- i went and got myself a masters degree in mechanical engineering! figuring i could convince myself i was worth something, and that it would solve my depression, but it didn't. i thought i was just burnt out, so i gave myself some time. about 15 years of time, more or less. the *universe* (who, in my world, is the attribution i give to the energy essence that runs things -- my spiritual "god", if you will) decided to throw us a global pandemic, and then a house fire, and not to mention the regular stuff that a budding adult goes through in their 20s. \[relationship baggage, health issues, parental health issues, grief] all that good stuff, you name it. 

it turns out, depression wasn't ruining my life. trying to fit my neurodivergent ass into neurotypical boxes was actually what was my depression was, and trying so hard to do it was what was ruining my life. depression was the thing telling my brain that something was wrong, but it took a lot of reflection and time to identify the thing that was actually wrong, for me. i spent a lot of time trying to figure out my depression because, i thought, if i could *just* do that, then i'd have a shot at navigating life like a normal person. i've come to understand that i was trying to navigate life like a **neurotypical** person, and that simply just doesn't work for me.

i've been meaning to put together a [YouTube playlist](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BzhbAK1pdPM&list=PLdzFUz2WpbSrqxydeTV2iS72NonnKco8W&pp=gAQB) of videos and accounts that were helpful for me in understanding my feelings and struggles with adhd. particularly the bits about delayed emotional regulation, the trifecta of \[interest, novelty, motivation] (i think?) in executive functioning, the differences in brain processing, etc. it will probably grow and change as i add to it. but yeah check it out, maybe it'll help you too like it has for me! ask me something if you need clarification or whatever!

anyways, my train of thought ends here, for now, on this subject
